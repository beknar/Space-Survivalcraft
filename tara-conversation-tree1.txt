KAEL: "What did you have?"

├─ TARA: [gestures to the star charts covering the walls]
│  "Artifacts. Archaeological data. Star maps from an expedition 
│   in the deep outer sectors. Maps that lead to something... 
│   significant."
│  └─ KAEL: "What kind of significance?"
│     [Tara hesitates, gauging his reaction]
│
├─ TARA: [walks to a secured case, opens it carefully]
│  "Evidence of a civilization that predates all known spacefaring 
│   species. A civilization that may have... ruled this region of space."
│  └─ KAEL: [steps closer, intrigued]
│     "How far back are we talking?"
│        └─ TARA: "Tens of thousands of years. Maybe more. 
│                 The dating is difficult with artifacts this old."
│
└─ TARA: [turns away, troubled]
   "The locals in the outer sectors call them 'The Killers.' 
    That's all anyone knows. A name. A myth. A warning."
   └─ KAEL: [alert] "The Killers?"
      [He pulls out a data pad, begins taking notes]

TARA: [walks to a holographic display, activates it]
      "During our excavation, we found structures. Technology. 
       Things that shouldn't exist given the age of the sites. 
       Things that suggest an intelligence far beyond our current understanding."

KAEL: "And the raiders took your research?"

TARA: [bitter laugh]
      "They took the physical artifacts. The data cores. Equipment. 
       Everything portable. But they missed something crucial."
      [She points to the star charts]

TARA: "These maps. The ones showing the excavation sites. 
      The coordinates of where we found the artifacts. 
      Where we believe more remains."

KAEL: [understanding]
      "They left empty-handed?"

TARA: "Not quite. They left something behind. Devices. 
       Advanced tech that doesn't match the raiders' profile. 
       And the markings... they're corporate."
      [She retrieves a metallic device, heavily damaged]

KAEL: [recognizes it immediately]
      "That's... Kratos Corporation engineering."

TARA: [nods grimly]
      "Exactly. Which raised several questions:
       Why were corporate operatives masquerading as political dissidents?
       What does Kratos want with ancient alien archaeology?
       And why would they abandon their own equipment?"

KAEL: "You're suggesting Kratos is searching for The Killers."

├─ TARA: [sits, exhausted]
│  "I'm suggesting Kratos is afraid of them. 
│   They didn't come for knowledge. They came for containment. 
│   Or destruction. And they failed."
│  └─ KAEL: [sits across from her]
│     "Failed how?"
│     └─ TARA: "They didn't find what they were looking for. 
│              Because they didn't have the star maps. 
│              I kept those hidden."
│        [Kael leans back, processing]
│
├─ TARA: "What if The Killers aren't extinct? What if they're dormant?
│         What if Kratos knows something about them that terrifies them?"
│  └─ KAEL: "That's speculation."
│     └─ TARA: [intense] "Is it? Then explain why a corporation 
│               would risk military operations to suppress archaeology."
│
└─ TARA: "The artifacts we found tell a story. A story of expansion. 
         Control. And then... silence. Complete, absolute silence. 
         As if an entire civilization simply... ceased."
   └─ KAEL: "Ceased? You mean—"
      └─ TARA: "I mean we don't know. That's the terrifying part.
               We don't know what happened to them. 
               And if Kratos is this desperate to prevent that knowledge 
               from spreading... maybe we should be terrified too."

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

KAEL: "Do you know what Kratos wants? Specifically?"

TARA: [retrieves multiple data pads, spreads them out]
      "Based on the sites they targeted, the artifacts they took, 
       and the device they left behind, I've reconstructed their search pattern."

KAEL: "And?"

TARA: [pulls up a 3D star map, highlights several sectors]
      "They're looking for a central location. A hub. 
       What I believe was the capital world of The Killers' civilization.

KAEL: "How do you know that?"

TARA: [points to ancient symbols on the device]
      "These markings. I've seen them before. On artifacts we recovered 
       from three separate sites spanning hundreds of light-years. 
       They appear to be directional markers. Navigation aids. 
       All pointing inward. All converging."

KAEL: [stands, paces]
      "On what?"
      [Tara brings up the star map, focuses on a particular region]

TARA: "A sector the locals call the 'Dead Zone.' Nothing survives there, 
      they say. Ships disappear. Signals die. Space itself seems hostile."

KAEL: "Hostile how?"

├─ TARA: "Gravitational anomalies. Radiation bursts. Energy patterns 
│        that don't match any natural phenomenon we know.
│        Like something is actively... resisting intrusion."
│  └─ KAEL: [darkly] "Or defending itself."
│     └─ TARA: [nods slowly]
│        "Yes. That's what I've been thinking too."
│
├─ TARA: "If The Killers built technology that can persist for tens 
│        of thousands of years, what kind of defensive systems 
│        might they have constructed?"

│     "Systems that could still be active."
│        └─ TARA: "Potentially. We won't know until someone goes there."
│
└─ TARA: "Kratos wants to reach that hub. I believe they want to

   └─ KAEL: "Then why haven't they succeeded?"
      └─ TARA: [slight smile]

      They know something we don't. And they're trying to control the narrative

KAEL: "Like you."

TARA: [nods] "I was supposed to die in that raid. My entire crew

KAEL: "Do you think they'll try again?"

KAEL: [joins her at the window]

TARA: [turns to face him, serious]

├─ TARA: "Kratos uses brute force. Military ships. Armed teams. 
│        They go in looking for resources to take or secrets to steal.
│        That's a different approach than mine."
│  └─ KAEL: "Which is?"
│     └─ TARA: [intense determination]

├─ TARA: [returns to her work, begins organizing her data]
